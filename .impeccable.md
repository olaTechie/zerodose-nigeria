# Design Context

## Users

The website serves three audiences with different goals but a shared need for rigorous, defensible evidence:

1. **Public-health programme managers** at the National Primary Health Care Development Agency (NPHCDA), Gavi country offices, and UNICEF — they arrive with a specific operational question (which intervention combination, in which community type, by which timeline) and need actionable answers without wading through an academic paper. They will use the site on a desktop in an office, often during a working session with colleagues.

2. **Academic researchers in vaccine epidemiology** — they want to inspect the methodology, verify the numbers against the underlying outputs, and decide whether the framework transfers to their own setting. They will arrive after reading the Vaccine paper or via a citation; they expect a Methods explorer, downloadable data, and a clear separation between findings and methods.

3. **Nigerian state-level policy stakeholders** (commissioners, state-level NPHCDA leads) — they need a defensible summary they can show to the governor's office or the federal coordinator. They will use the site once or twice and need to leave with one slide's worth of takeaway and a way to reach the supporting detail.

The shared job: **translate the integrated ML + ABM + CNA pipeline findings into operational guidance with audit trail intact**.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **rigorous, geographic, urgent**.

- **Rigorous** — every number on screen has a citable source. The interface should look like the people who built it can defend every claim under peer review. Confidence intervals visible. Methodological choices acknowledged inline rather than hidden.
- **Geographic** — Nigeria is the protagonist, not a backdrop. Maps and zone-level patterns are the primary visual language; charts come second. Place names in Hausa/Igbo/Yoruba transliterations where appropriate.
- **Urgent** — 36.8 percent national zero-dose prevalence is a present crisis. The interface should not feel decorative or self-satisfied. Restraint in palette and motion implies seriousness, but the *typography* and *information density* should communicate that this matters now.

The interface should evoke **considered conviction** — calm presentation, decisive content. Visitors should leave with one or two specific things they want to do differently, not just impressed by the visualisation craft.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Reference:** Our World in Data and Pew Research — editorial data-journalism in which the chart is the hero, prose drives narrative, the palette is restrained, and typography carries the seriousness. Specifically: confident sans-serif (or serif/sans pair) for body, scrollytelling sequences that are choreographed but not flashy, charts that are legible without colour-coding tricks.

**Anti-references:** corporate dashboard templates (Tableau-default-look), Bootstrap-era card grids, glassmorphism / neon-on-dark "AI tech" aesthetic, and McKinsey-style gradient-laden infographic decks. The site should not look like it could equally be selling a SaaS product or a vaccine intervention.

**Theme:** **Light by default**. Public-sector and academic audiences read in office settings, often print sections, and expect a calm light palette. A dark-mode toggle is acceptable but not required; if shipped, it should be a calm slate-on-charcoal, not a glow-on-black aesthetic.

**Geographic palette:** Nigeria-themed greens (current `primary: #006633` is appropriate) tied to the national flag, with gold/amber as the warm accent (`#e6a817`). Avoid garish brand greens; tint UI surfaces toward the brand hue at very low chroma (≤ 0.01) for cohesion. Coverage-status colours (on-track, at-risk, critical, crisis) and zone codes (NW, NE, NC, SE, SS, SW) are already tokenised — preserve and refine.

**Motion appetite — maximalist:** the Story page should feel like a choreographed Our World in Data narrative — staggered reveals, scroll-driven chart transitions, intentional parallax on the hero. The Policy and Explorer pages can be quieter. All motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion` and degrades gracefully.

## Design Principles

1. **Source-of-truth visible.** Every number on screen has a citable source: a hover-revealed source pointer, a footnote link, or an inline data-export. If a value is a posterior median, the credible interval is one click away. The audit trail is part of the brand.

2. **Geography first.** Maps, zone breakdowns, and state-level mosaics outweigh tables. When a finding is geographic, lead with the map; the chart is the supporting evidence, not the headline.

3. **Calm hierarchy, decisive content.** Spacing, weight, and a small accent palette do the work of organising the page. No more than two surface levels deep. Information density is welcome where it earns its keep (Methods, Explorer); the Story and Landing pages can breathe.

4. **Choreographed but not theatrical.** Motion is purposeful: a scroll reveal that builds an argument, a chart entrance that lets the eye land on the right point. No bounce, no parallax for parallax's sake, no decorative flourishes that the next visitor cannot reproduce by reading the source code.

5. **Accessible by default.** WCAG 2.2 AA target. All chart colour codings have a non-colour redundancy (pattern, label, position). Reduced-motion users get the full information without the choreography. Print stylesheet ships with the build.

## Technical context (non-design constraints, for reference)

- **Stack:** React 19 + Vite 8 + Tailwind v4 + framer-motion 12 + react-scrollama + granular d3-* packages (no full d3 bundle). Deployment via GitHub Pages at `https://olaTechie.github.io/zerodose-nigeria/`.
- **Routes:** Landing, Story (scrollytelling), Policy (interactive maps + scenario comparison), Explorer (7 analysis tabs).
- **Data:** Pre-generated JSON in `public/data/` from pipeline outputs (`scripts/prepare_website_data.py`, `precompute_scenarios.py`).
- **Existing tokens:** `primary` (Nigeria green family), `gold`, semantic `coverage` (on-track / at-risk / critical / crisis), zonal `zone.*` (nw, ne, nc, se, ss, sw). These are good — refinement may be needed but the architecture is sound.
